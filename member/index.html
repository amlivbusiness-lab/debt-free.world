❄️ Snowball vs Avalanche

❄️ Snowball

Pay smallest debts first. Builds momentum and quick wins. Best for motivation.

🔥 Avalanche

Pay highest-interest debts first. Mathematically optimal. Saves the most money.

The honest answer: Avalanche saves more money. Snowball saves more motivation. Both beat doing nothing by 10×.

Typical result: Avalanche vs Snowball

On €15,000 of mixed debt at 15–25% interest with €400/month payments, Avalanche typically saves €800–€1,400 in interest. The debt-free date is 2–4 months earlier.

🗺️ Debt Collection Process Map

Stage 1 — Missed payment

You miss a payment. Creditor sends a reminder. No legal action yet. Your right: contact them immediately to negotiate.

Stage 2 — Debt collection agency

After 30–90 days, debt may be sold to a collector. Your right: request debt validation in writing within 30 days.

Stage 3 — Formal demand letter

Official legal-sounding letter arrives. Still pre-legal. Your right: negotiate a settlement (often 40–60% of original). Use the AI letter generator above.

Stage 4 — Court proceedings

Creditor files a claim. Do not ignore court notices. Seek legal aid in your country immediately.

Stage 5 — Enforcement / bailiff

Court order obtained. Wages or assets may be seized. Even at this stage you can negotiate with the enforcement authority.

Stage 6 — Statute of limitations

Debts expire after 3–10 years depending on country. The clock resets if you make a payment or acknowledge the debt in writing.

📅 Monthly Updates

EU Debt Collection Directive — Changes Effective June 2026

New EU rules limit collector contact to 3 times per week maximum. Written contact must include the total amount claimed with full fee breakdown.

May 2026

Finland: KELA Livelihood Support — Expanded Eligibility

KELA expanded eligibility for debt-related hardship support. If housing debt exceeds 40% of net income, you may now qualify regardless of employment status.

April 2026

Klarna & Afterpay — New Settlement Policy

Both platforms now accept lump-sum settlements at 50–65% of outstanding balance through their hardship teams.
