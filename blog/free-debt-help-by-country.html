EU-wide rights every debtor should know

Collectors cannot: contact you outside 8am–9pm, threaten illegal action, contact your employer without a court order, add unauthorized fees, or claim debts past the statute of limitations (3–10 years by country).

You have the right to: request a payment plan in writing, dispute debt validity, request the original creditor's documentation, and be treated with dignity. Report violations to your national consumer protection authority.

Insolvency discharge: In most EU countries, personal insolvency provides a legal path to discharge remaining debt — typically within 3–6 years. You do not have to carry debt forever.

It is free by law in most EU countries and the UK. Counselors can reduce interest rates, stop collection calls, and set up plans creditors accept.
